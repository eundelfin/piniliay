Agriculture

  1. Push for self-sufficiency by ensuring adequate production of food for the population
  2. Expand and improve the production of export goods to transform agriculture into a reliable earner of foreign exchange
  3. Unburden farmers by removing irrigation and other government fees
  4. Support and subsidize farmers by ensuring that farmers are part of the overall economic development, that farmers are able to avail of crop insurance and farming implements.

Education

  1. Ensure free and comprehensive education from the basic to the tertiary level
  2. Allocate 65 billion pesos a year to provide free uniforms, books, and school supplies to almost 20 million public school students nationwide
  3. Provide the necessary infrastructure and facilities for education
  4. Enjoin schools to produce graduates whose skills match the demands of the industry 5. Intensify financial literacy programs to teach OFW families how to invest, and not to merely spend the money sent home.

Healthcare

  1. Ensure that every barangay across the country has a health center
  2. Promote nutrition and healthy lifestyle programs and projects by gathering stakeholders and establish a comprehensive healthy lifestyle program
  3. Provide access to free medicines and hospitalization.

Poverty

  1. Continue and expand the implementation of the conditional cash transfer program to include 60-64 years old Filipinos
  2. Create more stable jobs and livelihood opportunities for the people in the cities, and even more so in the countryside with focus on agriculture, manifacturing, and the BPO industry
  3. Provide more meaningful income for workers
  4. Provide more adequate and affordable food for every Filipino by raising agricultural productivity
  5. Dedicate more funds to support or subsidize small businesses and teach and help them grow.

Tax Reforms

  1. Remove tax deductions for Filipino workers earning Php 30,000 and below.
